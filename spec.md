# Dish Diary — Tech Spec

## One sentence
Dish Diary helps food lovers document their meals by automatically writing captions for food photos and organizing them into a personal blog.

## The problem
Food lovers eat at amazing restaurants and cook great meals, but writing captions is annoying and time-consuming — so photos just pile up in a camera roll, never shared or organized. There's no easy way to keep a personal food journal that looks good and actually says something interesting about each dish.

## How it works
- **Input:** User uploads a photo of food from their computer, plus an optional short note (e.g. "tacos from that spot on Main St")
- **Processing:** AI looks at the note and generates a fun, food-lover-style caption for the photo
- **Output:** The photo + caption get added to a blog page, organized by date, newest first

## APIs and tools needed
- [x] AI model (via Replit) — writes captions for each food photo based on the user's note
- [ ] Discord — not needed
- [ ] Gmail / email — not needed
- [ ] Google Calendar — not needed
- [ ] Weather API — not needed
- [ ] News API — not needed

## What the user sees (frontend)
- **Blog page (`/`):** A grid of food photos with their AI-written captions and the date posted. Newest photos at the top.
- **Upload page (`/upload`):** A simple form to pick a photo from their computer, add an optional note, and hit "Post."

## What happens behind the scenes (backend)
- `GET /` — loads all posts from storage and renders the blog page
- `GET /upload` — shows the upload form
- `POST /upload` — receives the photo file + note, saves the photo to `static/uploads/`, calls `ask_ai()` to generate a caption, saves the post data (filename, caption, date) to `posts.json`, redirects to the blog
- Data is stored in `posts.json` (a list of posts, each with: filename, caption, date)
